💰 What Is Money?

Money is a universally accepted medium of exchange. Instead of exchanging goods directly through barter, money allows people to buy and sell efficiently. Today, money exists in many forms including cash, bank balances and digital payments.

💡 Key Insight

Money itself has little value unless people trust it. That trust comes from governments, banks and the economy.

🌍 Why Does Money Matter?

Money makes trade efficient, helps people save for the future, and enables businesses and governments to operate. It also allows us to measure the value of goods and services consistently.

🤔 Did You Know?

India is one of the world's leaders in digital payments. Millions of UPI transactions are processed every day, making payments faster and more convenient for individuals and businesses.

🧠 Knowledge Check

Which of the following is considered a primary function of money?

💡 Answer: B. Money acts as a Medium of Exchange, allowing people to buy and sell goods efficiently.

✅ Key Takeaways

  • Money replaced the barter system.
  • Money is accepted because people trust it.
  • Money acts as a medium of exchange.
  • Money stores value over time.
  • Digital payments are transforming finance.
